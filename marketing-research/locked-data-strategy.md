# Locked-Data / Registration Strategy — Gating Matrix

Principle (from the benchmark winners): **show the headline, gate the depth; show the sample, gate the save.** The lock must always be next to visible real data proving what's behind it (GuruFocus/MarketChameleon pattern), and the account must be a legible deal (Sharesight pattern). Never gate a crawlable SEO page (Macrotrends/stockanalysis lesson; Wallmine anti-lesson).

## Tier model

- **Anonymous:** headline data, top 3–5 of every list, blurred rows with real symbols visible, one limited tool run, one full sample, all SEO pages.
- **Registered (free):** full tables, saves, alerts, full explanations, custom inputs, share/export, digest.
- **Pro (later, honest):** speed (faster refresh), depth (history, advanced filters), volume (more alerts/exports), and premium research. Never move a previously-free thing behind Pro without a grandfather note.

## Anti-sleaze rules (make locking feel useful, not scammy)

1. Every lock names its price: "free account" or "Pro" — no mystery CTAs.
2. Blur real data, never fake data. If the pipeline is down, hide the module; never blur a placeholder.
3. One lock style, one padlock icon, everywhere (design-system token), small and quiet.
4. No entry popups, no exit-intent, no countdown discounts, no "only 3 spots left."
5. Locks never move backwards: what's free stays free, or you tell people why.
6. Every locked module keeps its disclaimers visible even when blurred (delayed data, not advice).
7. The signup modal returns the user to the exact unlocked thing (see registration-funnel.md).
