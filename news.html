How the AI news feed works

We cover every headline that touches the ~83-name AI stock universe plus AI ETFs — but we do not trust the feed a story arrived on. Vendor per-ticker feeds are noisy (an Nvidia feed carries syndicated macro stories that have nothing to do with Nvidia), so every story is re-tagged by which of our companies its headline actually names — by ticker ($NVDA, "(NVDA)") or by company name. Stories that name none of our companies are dropped.

What that gets you

  • Accurate ticker tags — a headline pitting Amazon against Microsoft is tagged to both, not to whatever feed it happened to appear in.
  • Deduplication — the same syndicated story collapses to one entry, tagged with every company it names and its sector.
  • Timestamps on every item; the feed refreshes through the trading day.
